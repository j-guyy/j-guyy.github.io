The Adventure

I was working in Sydney for a few weeks, and one of my weekends was spent in the Snowy Mountains of the Great Dividing Range. Destination: Mount Kosciuszko, the Australia high point.

Thredbo Closing Day

Snow was present but not terrible ‚Äî sounds like this year was a very low snow winter for them, which worked out in my favor since I had no flotation, just microspikes. I started the climb from the Thredbo ski resort, which had very little snow. Made it up to the top of the resort to find the liftees throwing a party for closing day.

The Kosciuszko Walk

I walked along the Kosciuszko Walk, a surprisingly flat footpath, to the base of Kosciuszko proper. This mountain range is so strange ‚Äî it's steep at the bottom, flat like a plateau up top, with the occasional black rock, snowy peak jutting up. Unlike anything I'd hiked before.

Victory

Got to the top of Kosciuszko and glided my way down the snowy hills. Man, I wish I had brought my backcountry skis for this top part.

On the way down, I noticed my big toe on my right foot was really starting to feel some pain on the ascents and descents. I decided to start going to PT when I got back to the US, but had no idea what was causing it. Would later find out it was a stress fracture of the sesamoid, get it misdiagnosed as a metatarsal injury, treat it incorrectly for a year, and then need surgery when the stress fracture became a fully fragmented bone. But that's a story for another day.
