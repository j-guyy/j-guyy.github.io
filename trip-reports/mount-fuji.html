The Adventure

The best parts of Japan are outside of Tokyo. I took a series of trains from Shibuya all the way out to Fujiyoshida, where my Airbnb host picked me up from the train station.

The Airbnb in Fujiyoshida didn't have any bed ‚Äî you sleep on a pad on the floor. No chairs either ‚Äî you kneel at the table. When in Japan.

The Climb

I chose this Airbnb due to its proximity to the base of the mountain. I woke up at 1 AM and walked to Station 1 to begin my climb. Most people get a headstart by taking a bus all the way up to Station 5, but I wanted the full experience.

The first stretch was through a foggy forest in the middle of the night. I saw some deer, and then heard squealing nearby. I turned down my headlamp and watched a boar poke its nose out of the tall grass right next to the trail before it ran back in. That was spooky.

Off-Season Rules

Technically, I was climbing before the season started, so there was a big gate blocking the trail above the 5th station. Easy enough to walk behind the gate and keep going.

Further up, I passed a few other people climbing the mountain in off season. I met an old man who claimed to be "Mr. Fuji." He said, "I Mister Fuji, I climb Mount Fuji one thousand time!" I smiled and kept moving. Come to find out later, after looking him up, he's actually an insane mountaineer.

To the Top

I had brought microspikes, an ice axe, and crampons just in case ‚Äî none of them were needed. The snow was minimal and the route was clean. I fueled the whole way on rice balls and jellies from the local 7-Eleven. Japan's convenience stores are unreal.

Victory

Summited Fuji as the morning light filled the sky. The snow was still deep inside the crater, and I could see some skin tracks and turns. Someone must have brought their backcountry skis... man that would be cool. I enjoyed the view for a few minutes, then ran the whole way down along dirt switchbacks, letting gravity do the work. I made it back to the Airbnb around 11am, stumbled in, and crashed on the sleeping pad. What a time.
