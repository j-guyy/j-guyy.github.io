The Adventure

I was on an Ecuador trail running trip with Runners of the Sage in early August of 2023. Viejo Nacho, our mountain guide, brought us all to Cotopaxi National Park in a bus. Cotopaxi itself is blocked off from mountaineering because it's currently active, but its neighbor Sincholagua was fair game.

The Ascent

The hike brought us up from a forest, through high-altitude plains (which reminded me of Wyoming), and eventually into the volcanic alpine. The landscape shifted dramatically with every thousand feet of gain ‚Äî lush green giving way to barren rock and volcanic terrain.

Some of the group had to head down around the 12,000-foot mark, showing signs of altitude sickness. Those of us who were feeling okay pushed on.

The Final Pitch

We went on belay for the final pitch ‚Äî a climb up to the summit ridge. The exposure was real, but the rope and the team kept things moving.

It was brutally cloudy at the summit, so no views above 16,000 feet. Sometimes nature just keeps the curtain drawn.

Victory

We caught awesome views of Cotopaxi on the descent, though ‚Äî the active volcano towering nearby, a reminder of just how alive this landscape is.
