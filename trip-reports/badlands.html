The Adventure

I had just wrapped up a few days exploring the Black Hills — summiting Black Elk Peak, driving the Needles Highway, and visiting Mount Rushmore and Crazy Horse. With my gravel bike riding along in the back of the car, it felt like a crime not to use it.

The Ride

I parked along Highway 240 and set off from Ancient Hunters Overlook, riding out to Panorama Point and back. The plan was simple: stop at every overlook and soak it all in.

The Badlands are unlike anywhere else. The formations rise up out of the prairie like something from another planet — jagged spires, layered buttes, and endless eroded ridgelines stretching to the horizon. Riding through it rather than just driving felt like the right way to experience the scale of the place.

Bison

Partway through the ride, a small herd of bison were hanging out right alongside the road. Not a distant dot on the horizon — right there. I slowed down, gave them plenty of space, and kept moving. Hard to beat that kind of wildlife encounter from a bike.

Onward

Quick adventure, but a good one. After the ride it was back in the car and on to the next stop: Devils Tower.
