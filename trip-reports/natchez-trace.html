The Adventure

My first multi-day bike tour. I drove from Houston to Natchez, Mississippi and crashed in town for the night. Woke up the next morning ready to roll. Ate hotel breakfast ‚Äî eggs. Yikes, first time I'd had animal protein in months.

Heading North

I drove to Walmart, grabbed the supplies I needed ‚Äî including a tarp to wrap all my stuff in to keep it dry, since it was supposed to start pouring ‚Äî and parked the car. It was game time. I grabbed Tony, my clunky-yet-reliable old road bike, packed my backpack, and began heading north up the Natchez Trace, no clear destination in mind.

The Trace itself is 444 miles long, so I knew I wouldn't be completing it. I was not in cycling shape and only brought one change of clothes. I was in this for the touring experience.

I rode along the winding road, clouds overhead and a slight breeze. I was ready for the rain to start at any moment, but it didn't. I made it all the way out to Port Gibson, where I exited the Trace and looked for a place to stay.

The Plantation

There were no hotels in town, nor Airbnbs, but there was a listing for a bed and breakfast on Google Maps. I called the number and sure enough, they had an opening for the night.

I rode up to the address just as the rain started. And man, did it start. I also noticed that the property was massive. What was this place? I met the owner and found out that it used to be a plantation, but now it was owned by the descendants of the slaves who worked it. What a cool story.

I headed to my room, soaking wet, and crashed for the night.

The Ride Back

I woke up the next morning, ate breakfast with the plantation owner, and began my ride back to the car. Cold and windy, and my butt hurt, but I made it back to Natchez and drove on back to Houston.
