The Adventure

This adventure did not go as expected, but I still made the best of it.

I was coming off a work trip in China and hopped a flight to Jeju-do, an island off the south coast of South Korea ‚Äî basically the Korean Hawaii. I came here for one reason: this is where the country high point is.

Getting Settled

Fresh off the work trip, laundry was priority number one. Step one: go to a bank and withdraw local currency. Step two: go to the grocery store and pay in won ‚Äî get change in coins. Step three: use the coins from the grocery store to pay for laundry. After those three steps, I was ready to roll.

I rented a road bike and stayed at a hotel right on the water. I used my bike to get around, and found an awesome outdoor gym right nearby my hotel. My first day in Jeju was spent just taking it all in.

The Night Before

The night before my birthday, I headed into a small seafood restaurant right on the water's edge. The people working there didn't speak any English, so I pointed to the fish I wanted on the menu, but told them I couldn't afford what was listed. They still set me up with whatever I was willing to pay for.

I asked the owner, using Google Translate, if she gets a lot of Americans in there. She said no, very rarely. I stuffed myself with excellent fresh fish and called it a night.

Birthday Morning

The plan for my birthday was ambitious: dip my rental bike tire in the East China Sea, bike the 8 miles and 1,900 feet of gain up to the Halla-san trailhead, stash the bike, hike the remaining 4,500 feet to the summit, then cruise back down to the sea on two wheels. Sea to summit and back. Everything was slated to work. Until it wasn't.

I woke up at 4 AM and discovered a pinch flat in my bike tire. The bike shop wasn't open yet, so I couldn't rush there to get a new tube. I tried to call a Kakao taxi to bring me to the trailhead, but nothing was nearby. So I strapped on my running vest and began running on the road uphill toward the base of the mountain. Eventually I made it to downtown where I could hail a taxi. The driver brought me up to the Halla-san trailhead just in time for my timed entry into the park ‚Äî I only had a two-hour window during which I could enter.

The Climb

I climbed Halla-san in brutal heat. It was beautiful and lush, with large vertical cliffs jutting up above the forest. The trail was well marked and there were a surprising number of people up there. Looking into the volcano's crater at the summit was very cool, and then it was onto the descent! New country high point, check.

Victory

After descending from the summit and returning to the trailhead, I grabbed a taxi back to my hotel and then it was time to relax. I also made my way to the bike shop that evening to get my tube replaced. Such a bummer that that part didn't work out. Looking back at the climb, it was definitely not the hardest mountain I've climbed, nor the most unique mountain challenge, but it was still a birthday adventure to remember.

But the Jeju trip wasn't over. The bike was fixed, and the island was calling ‚Äî time to ride around Jeju.
