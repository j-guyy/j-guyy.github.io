The Adventure

After my Halla-san adventure took an unexpected turn, it was time to make the most of my last day on Jeju-do. I tracked down a spare tube at a local bike shop, pumped up the tires, and headed out.

Along the North Shore

I rode out to a little outdoor gym perched above the East China Sea — a solid first stop — then continued east along the island's north shore toward Hamdeok Beach. The sea breeze was steady, the road hugged the coast, and Halla-san loomed in the distance the whole way out. Hard to ask for a better backdrop.

Hamdeok Beach

Hamdeok did not disappoint. An outdoor market, a massive beach, and water that was the most beautiful shade of blue. There were a ton of people out enjoying it — the kind of busy beach energy that's infectious. I locked up the bike and plunged right in. The water was a perfect temperature, and exactly what the day called for.

I grabbed some snacks from the market, dried off, and took my time before getting back on the bike for the return ride to Jeju-si.

Back to the City

The ride back was just as good as the ride out. Returned the bike at the end of the day and caught a flight up to Seoul — apparently the most flown route in the world. Jeju delivered.
